Best advises not to get stuck in design system

Best advises not to get stuck in design system

Nowadays we may see, how important design is. More and more, both sharks of business world and small start ups trying to involve their costumer thanks to designing. Still, many designers don’t know the perfect way of working with different systems that’s why this list may be amazingly useful for those, who is interested in high-quality product.

Design as a A class fuel for project

At first glance benefits of creating a design system are obvious and clear. It’s pretty easy to connect a design system to cost savings, scalability, efficiency, and consistency. Somehow, even seeing such benefits from design systems, many, fail to provide necessary resources for a successful system. Most likely it happens because teams feel pressure while launching some features, and those resources, spent on the design system, shall decrease velocity on the product that pays the bills. If you really want to make savings thanks to a design system then it can not be treated as an secondary something, not like just useless background. As the company grew, and teams began developing product, future customers ask for concrete UI themes, and, basically, thats how new product lines were born. From the start the design system was looked as a saving favor, it drives across these new teams and products, and allows to quickly theme our UI. And soon leaders understood that without devoted developers, the design system would never become something more than sketches of symbols. For those, who thinks about design system only as about many symbols, must be explained that it has its own unique set of end users, who have their own unique problems and contexts of use. Each step crave pensive systems and capable guidance between stakeholders. For a design system to be successful, there's a rule -- you must have a team whose only job is to work only on it.

The way to successes is team

The easiest way to build system is if you have support from the people who are sitting in expensive chairs in offices. But the hardest part is to get your c-level team to ideal of idea, especially because of given resources, needed to build a helpful and pracious design system. All you need to get started on that kind of a design system is one designer and one developer who are passionate enough to work, improve and work again.

Work with team as a good leader not selfish boss

...As stated above, there is an obvious benefit to the design system, but even picking up the right one, the team does not always want to use it. Some of the designers may have formed some behavior earlier and used the opposite design systems before, so expect that the pressure through the "in-house tool" will not flow successfully and may even impair the performance of the future product. The design system should not only add value to the company, but also simplify lives of the people who do the work. Otherwise, the team will copy inconsistent elements from random corners of one product, create incomprehensible designs, complicate the array of one design system with another. To facilitate the work of the team, it is necessary to involve it in the construction of the design system. It is advisable to talk to each project creator and find out which systems they have used before, or how their experience will help in creating a new one. The workflow of the team should be understood. After that, the work will begin to go on its own with completely smaller and simpler problems. Just because the design system is primarily an internal tool it doesn't mean that you do not have to confirm it. A design system is completive archive of various data so don't mess up with it Although many design systems show a whole library of UI components and branding guidelines, a true design system includes so much more. In case of an absence of integration of design system with the products it supports, then its value becomes severely limited. Many teams are trying to create an upgrade of design so that changes may apply to all products, including the library of items. However, thi s is completely impossible for now, and then you have to make manual adjustments. Yes, in the case of a widespread element change in the product, again, it is necessary to check the integrity of all layouts. Some changes may be accidental, or involuntarily missed items. The design system should outline a clear process for introducing new components and models to change existing ones. And this process must match the size, culture and maturity of your business. Small teams can get along with unofficial, impromptu weakened messages, while larger teams may need official reviews and change requests. Use new components and new ways of doing things Once your design system will reach the point which will look like a critical mass with a large enough amount of components possibly satisfying to solve most, if not all, design problems. At the same time, design systems make reusable components too readily accessible. One design teams can use or imitate existing components without solving how a new component might work on the design problem. Teams also tend to overplay the importance of usage of a basic easier solution because it is similar to an existing pattern which most probably was used before, and not for once. Designers must constantly ask themselves if a new component will be able to solve the problem better — whether we are okay with simply satisfying the users’ needs. To become one of the best in A list company must developing without stop, delighting users in ways that competitors can't. Don't make empty discussions, system always works The hardest thing is to convince people to adopt your design system. But at the same time for some systems, the biggest challenge is to get stakeholders to consensus on design principles and how components work.Often teams tend to overthink how a component should function, creating unbelievable and useless scenarios. Worst part is clinging to small parts of work that suppose to be thought almost in the end of any project. Spending time on unplanned steps will decline productivity of each doer and stakeholder and financial looses as well. The main point of a design system is to make difficult components easier, simplified. So instead of having too many conversations about a hypothetical future, making new hard components, remind everyone that you can talk and discuss the design system any other time.Choose the responsible person for project management Do not expect that each member of your team will be perfectly aware and remember about their accurate role in the project, so it’s best to appoint a responsible person who perfectly knows about each change and new idea, will constantly remind and motivate doer to take the right actions. Do not be afraid that design might start changing, because an important element of each design is the synergy between each from stuff who involved in project. In ideal, you should make "change notification”, about which the responsible designer will know and be able to explain new tasks and manage the project with adjustments. Make from novice professionals When hiring new employees, don't think that they have necessarily worked in any design system. Not to spoil all the further work, better to let the freshers importance of their being in current position, and after that YOU, having got a perfectly adapted to your style of work professional, shall only see the incredible advantages of this process. Do not be afraid to type in a professional vocabulary and create it in the workplace and staple of everyday communication, as this will most likely lead to quick understanding and better work. A nice bonus will be an improvement of professional skills and work experience. Make curves not flatness Although design systems are newsworthy, we should warn you! In case if you are small company with less than five products and a little costumers, then you’d better to wait for development and only after start creating design systems. Big companies are most likely to get more benefits from designing, for small it is inappropriate. Design systems suppose to make everyt hing easier so there’s no meaning to simplify already onefold things. ...
